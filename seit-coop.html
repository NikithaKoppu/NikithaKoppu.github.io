Visit online

In this period, I learned a lot about what the industry was like and specifically what startup environments are like, a fast-paced, community dynamic. I came to enjoy this as I got to be a integral part of the product life cycle, I knew what other engineers were working on, and what the customers would say specifically about feature improvements that I had a part in. I was one of two SEITs in the company and I had just as much responsibility as my mentor. We would split regression testing, automation efforts, monitor smoke, create test plans, etc for every release and feature that went out the door.

Role
Software Engineer in Test Co-op

The first project that I was given to work on my own team independently was verifying a new feature of audit events that tracked enforcement decisions based on user policies. I was the sole SEIT on the team with two developers looking for my input and feedback on the schema, appearance, and verification. I created a framework for the automation tests for both windows and linux agents all the while working closely with the devs to verify their day to day feature improvements. Once the framework was in place, the test plan came to life in the automation and my pace for feedback rapidly increased. The tests were put into smoke to verify consistency and the feature was released. It was very satisfying to know that my team was able to create this and we worked so well together in the process.
